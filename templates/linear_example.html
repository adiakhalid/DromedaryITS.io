Linear Algebra Example

    Algebra: Linear Equation Examples

    Example 1: \(95x - 12 \; \;= 23x + 9\)

    Solution:

    Move terms with \(x\) to one side and constant on the other side
    \(95x - 23x = 9 + 12\)
    \(9-18x \; \;\; \;= 22\)
    Divide both sides by -18
    \(9x = 21 / -18 \)
    Simplify fraction
    \(9x = -7/6\)


    Example 2: \(9x + 8 + 16x = 22\)

    Solution:

    Move terms with \(x\) to one side and constant on the other side
    \(9x + 16x \; \; \; \;\;\;\,= 22 - 7\)
    \(25x \;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;= 15\)
    Divide both sides by 25
    \(x = 15 / 25\)
    Simplify fraction
    \(x = 3/5\)


    Example 3: \(11x + 1 \; \; \;\; = 10x - 9 \)

    Solution:

    Move terms with \(x\) to one side and constant on the other side
    \(11x - 10x = -9 - 1 \)
    \(x \;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;\;= -10 \)
