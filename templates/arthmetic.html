Mathematics

    Arithmetic

    Arithmetic deals with four basic operations on numbers. The detailed discussions related to all four arithmetic operations are given below.

  • Addition

    The operation denoted by the symbol '+' is known as addition, which involves calculating the sum of two or more numbers. When two or more numbers are added together, it results in a single value.
    For example: \(279 + 123 = 402\).

  • Subtraction

    The subtraction operation, represented by '-', indicates the difference between two numbers, it involves subtracting one number from another. Subtraction is mostly used to determine what remains when things are taken away which is opposite of addition.
    For example: \(42 - 11 = 31\).

  • Multiplication

    Multiplication is known as repeated addition, symbolized by \('\times'\). Multiplication calculate the total when a number repeats itself several times. For instance, 4 times 2 equals 8, which can be expressed as \(4 \times 2 = 8\). The terms involved in multiplication are the multiplicand and the multiplier . The outcome of multiplying the multiplicand (4) and multiplier (2) is referred to as the product (8).
    For example: \(12 \times 12 = 144\).

  • Division

    Division involves splitting something into equal parts or groups and ensures equal sharing. Division is opposite of multiplication.
    For example, there are 4 boxes, and each box contains 5 apples which contain total 20 apples (i.e., \(4 \times 5 = 20\)). Now, take those 20 apples and divide them into 4 equal groups. Each group will have 5 apples (i.e., \(20 ÷ 4 = 5\)), here we are distributing a total quantity 20 into 4 equal parts.
